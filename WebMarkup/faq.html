Frequently Asked Questions

We’ve gathered some of the most common questions asked by those looking to quit smoking. From dealing with cravings to understanding the best strategies for quitting, find answers to your most pressing concerns here. If you don’t see your question, feel free to reach out — we’re here to help.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best methods for quitting smoking?

There are several effective methods for quitting smoking, including nicotine replacement therapy (NRT), medications like varenicline, behavioral therapy, and support from quit-smoking groups. Finding the right combination of tools that work for you is key.

How long do cravings last after quitting?

Cravings typically peak within the first week after quitting but can continue for a few weeks. Most people find that cravings reduce in intensity after the first 3-4 weeks. Staying busy, drinking water, and using nicotine replacements can help manage them.

How does smoking affect my health long-term?

Smoking is linked to serious health issues like lung cancer, heart disease, stroke, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). The sooner you quit, the sooner your body can begin to heal, and within a year of quitting, your risk of heart disease drops by half.

Can vaping help me quit smoking?

While some people use vaping as a tool to quit smoking, it's important to be cautious. Vaping is not without risks, and it’s best to use scientifically proven methods like NRT or behavioral therapy to quit smoking. Consult with a healthcare professional for guidance.

What should I do if I relapse and start smoking again?

Relapses are common when quitting smoking, and it's important not to get discouraged. Review what led to the relapse, learn from it, and set a new quit date. The more attempts you make, the more likely you are to succeed.
